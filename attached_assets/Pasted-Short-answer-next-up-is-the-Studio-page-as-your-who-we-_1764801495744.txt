## 1. Studio page – content & structure

Assume route: `/studio` → `client/src/pages/Studio.tsx` (or whatever you already use).

### 1.1 Page meta

Use `PageMeta` like on Process/Ventures:

```tsx
<PageMeta
  title="Studio"
  description="Verifiable Systems Group is a verification-first venture studio that designs, builds, and governs systems on non-custodial rails for institutions and ecosystems."
  preloadImage="/hero-texture.png"
/>
```

This hits: who you are, your specialty, and who you’re for (institutions + ecosystems) in one line, which is exactly what best-practice mission/vision guidance suggests.

---

### 1.2 Hero – “who we are” in one breath

**Overline:**

> Verifiable Systems Group

**Heading:**

> A verification-first venture studio.

**Body copy:**

```text
Verifiable Systems Group (VSG) is a small, focused studio that designs and operates systems where behaviour can be inspected, not guessed. We build on non-custodial rails, keep governance explicit, and treat every venture as an experiment that has to earn its place in the portfolio.
```

Underneath, a short supporting line to frame the rest of the page:

```text
The Studio is where we define our thesis, choose what to build, and decide how ventures like Helios, Stackme, and Lumina move from idea to governed infrastructure.
```

---

### 1.3 Section: “What we do”

This is basically your **service / capability map**, but described at a principles level (not “packages”).

**Heading:**

> What we do

Grid: 3 cards (`md:grid-cols-3`), each a simple shadcn `Card` with title + body.

1. **Systems Architecture & Research**

   ```text
   We map the real-world constraints around capital, identity, and regulation, then design systems that make those constraints explicit. That includes verification flows, risk boundaries, and the rails needed for institutions to stay in control.
   ```

2. **Venture Studio & Incubation**

   ```text
   We run a repeatable process for turning hypotheses into ventures. Each venture enters with a clear thesis, moves through our six-stage pipeline, and is either graduated into shared infrastructure or archived with its lessons preserved.
   ```

3. **Standards, Playbooks & Governance**

   ```text
   We turn what we learn into reusable standards: patterns, controls, and playbooks that can be applied across ventures. This is how we avoid solving the same problem twice and how external partners can evaluate how we work.
   ```

This maps very cleanly to how serious venture studios describe themselves: **thesis**, **venture-building process**, and **reusable playbooks/standards**.

---

### 1.4 Section: “Who we’re for”

This is for prospective partners, banks, infra providers, etc.

**Heading:**

> Who we’re for

Short intro:

```text
We exist for institutions and ecosystems that care about verification, not just velocity.
```

Bullets (or 3 horizontal chips):

* **Financial institutions & fintechs** — who need non-custodial, audit-ready rails instead of opaque black boxes.
* **Market and infrastructure providers** — building exchanges, marketplaces, and rails where authenticity, risk, or capital flows must be explainable.
* **Ecosystems & protocols** — that want to see real-world use-cases built on top of their primitives under conservative, governed conditions.

---

### 1.5 Section: “How the Studio is structured”

Link Studio → Ventures + Process in a single visual.

**Heading:**

> How the Studio is structured

Body:

```text
We keep the Studio small and focused. Most of the complexity lives in the ventures and the systems they rely on.
```

Then a 3-column “structure” block:

1. **The Studio**

   ```text
   A core team focused on thesis, architecture, standards, and governance. The Studio sets the bar for what we build and how risk is managed.
   ```

2. **Ventures**

   ```text
   Individual ventures like Helios, Stackme, and Lumina that explore specific opportunities. Each one competes for capital, attention, and risk budget.
   ```

3. **Standards & Library**

   ```text
   A growing set of standards, playbooks, and patterns that capture what works. This is how we avoid re-learning the same lesson in three different places.
   ```

Underneath, add two inline links:

* “See the venture portfolio → /ventures”
* “See the six-stage venture process → /process”

This matches best-practice venture studio storytelling: clearly explain **structure**, not just logos or buzzwords.

---

### 1.6 Section: “Our pillars” (summary, not the full handbook)

Here we surface your identity pillars in a **compressed** way, with a path to deeper pages later (Purpose/Vision/Mission/Pillars).

**Heading:**

> Our pillars

Intro:

```text
Underneath the ventures and tooling, the Studio is anchored on a small number of pillars. They show up in everything we design and every venture we approve.
```

3 bullets (match the identity doc we crafted conceptually):

1. **Verifiable Systems**

   ```text
   Critical behaviours must be observable, testable, and, where it matters, provable. We optimise for systems you can reason about under stress.
   ```

2. **Non-custodial Rails**

   ```text
   Wherever possible, we keep key and asset control with institutions and users. Our systems orchestrate workflows; they do not take custody.
   ```

3. **Governed Experiments**

   ```text
   We treat ventures as governed experiments with explicit limits, not permanent products. Risk budgets, constraints, and exit criteria are part of the design.
   ```

Below that, one line that sets up future pages:

```tsx
<p className="mt-4 text-sm text-muted-foreground">
  Our purpose, vision, and mission expand on these pillars and how we apply them over time.
</p>
```

Later we’ll hang `/purpose`, `/vision`, `/mission` pages off that sentence.
