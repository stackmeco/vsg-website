## 1. Why there’s no “Approach overview” item right now

Mechanically, it’s simple:

* For **Ventures**, your nav data includes an explicit **“All Ventures”** row, so the dropdown renderer treats that as the “hero” item.
* For **Studio**, the nav data includes a **“Studio”** row as the first child, so you get a highlighted overview row there too.
* For **Approach**, we only defined **Pillars, Process, Standards** as children — **no top-level “Approach” entry** in the dropdown data and no `/approach` page.

So the code is doing exactly what it was told: render the items it knows about. We just never gave it an “Approach overview” object to work with.

There’s no deep technical constraint; it’s an information-architecture gap.

---

## 2. Why that gap matters (UX + IA, not just aesthetics)

For B2B sites with complex stories, research is pretty consistent on a few points:

* **Top-level nav items with children should point to a summary/overview**, not be dead ends. Otherwise users are forced to know *exactly* which subpage they want.
* Good navigation **shows the hierarchy clearly** – parent vs child – and keeps that pattern consistent across the menu, especially on large/mega menus.
* Many high-performing mega menus add a **“View all / All X / Overview”** entry at the top of each column so users can zoom out instead of choosing a highly specific leaf by guess.
* UX authors specifically recommend including a **link to the top-level page inside the dropdown**, styled differently so it’s obviously the parent.

Right now:

* **Ventures** and **Studio** follow that pattern (overview first, then children).
* **Approach** breaks it (no overview row), which:

  * Increases cognitive load: “Do I want Pillars? Process? Standards? What’s the difference?”
  * Makes the IA feel less deliberate, especially to risk/ops people who *live* in “Approach” content.

So your intuition is correct: **Approach needs an overview row** if you want the nav to feel intentional and institutional.

---

You don’t have to change the click behaviour of the top bar label right now; just adding the overview row in the dropdown is enough to fix the visual and IA mismatch.
