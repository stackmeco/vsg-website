### **III. Visual & UX Audit ("The Control Room Vibe")**

You asked if transitions make it look cheap. [cite\_start]You have already tightened the `Sheet` and `Dialog` durations to `150ms`[cite: 1202, 1268]. **This is correct.**

However, there is a lingering visual issue in your CSS architecture.

#### **1. The "Elevation" Problem**

[cite\_start]In `index.css`, you defined an intricate `elevate` system using shadows (`shadow-xs`, etc.)[cite: 1427].

  * **Critique:** Shadows imply that UI elements are floating pieces of paper. This is the "Material Design" (Google) or "Stripe" aesthetic. It is soft and consumer-friendly.
  * **The Institutional Fix:** Control rooms use **Outlines** and **Background Opacity**.
      * [cite\_start]*Action:* In `ValueCard.tsx` [cite: 1350] [cite\_start]and `ProjectTile.tsx`[cite: 1081], remove `hover-elevate`.
      * *Replace with:* `hover:border-primary/50` and `hover:bg-card/80`.
      * *Why:* When you hover over a Bloomberg terminal component, it doesn't float up; it *glows* or gets sharper borders. This feels "electric" rather than "paper-like."

#### **2. Data Density in the Pipeline**

[cite\_start]Your `Pipeline.tsx` page uses large cards[cite: 1712].

  * **Critique:** Large cards with lots of padding feel like a brochure.
  * **The Institutional Fix:** Offer a "List View" or "Table View" for the pipeline projects. Tables suggest data management and rigor.
      * [cite\_start]*Action:* Consider using the `Table` component [cite: 1328] for the Pipeline page to list projects, current status, and "Last Updated" columns. This looks like a portfolio manager's screen.

#### **3. Accessibility & Contrast**

[cite\_start]In `StageChip.tsx`[cite: 1084], the "Research" variant uses `text-[hsl(var(--accent-warm))]` (Orange/Gold) on a transparent background.

  * **Risk:** Thin orange text on a dark grey background often fails WCAG accessibility standards.
  * **Solution:** Increase the font weight to `font-medium` or `font-semibold` for colored text on dark backgrounds to ensure legibility on non-retina screens.
