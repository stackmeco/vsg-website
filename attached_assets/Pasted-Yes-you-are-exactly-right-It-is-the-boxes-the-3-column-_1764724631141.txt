Yes, you are exactly right. It is the **boxes (the 3-column grid)**.

The "Industrial Control Room" aesthetic relies on rigid grids. When you try to put "Manifesto" style text (which has nuance and length) into a 3-column card designed for short stats, the text either **truncates (cuts off)** or the card stretches and breaks the visual rhythm.

The 3-column grid forces you to write "Soundbites."
You want to write "Philosophy."

To fix this without making the text "extreme" (too short), we need to **change the container, not just the text.**

### **The Design Fix: The "Bento" Layout**

Instead of squeezing all three (Purpose, Vision, Mission) into equal narrow columns, we will use a **2-over-1** layout:

  * **Row 1 (50/50 Split):** Purpose & Vision (Shorter text fits here).
  * **Row 2 (Full Width):** Mission (Longer text with bullet points gets room to breathe).

This solves the spacing issue instantly while keeping the "Control Room" look.
