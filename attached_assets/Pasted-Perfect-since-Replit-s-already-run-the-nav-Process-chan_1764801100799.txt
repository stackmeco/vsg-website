## 1. Ventures overview page (`/ventures`)

### 1.1 Role & tone

* Role: **Portfolio hub** for all ventures.
* Tone: Confident, forward-looking, slightly aspirational; more “lab / portfolio” than “feature list”.

### 1.2 Content model

Use the same basic layout pattern as `Studio` / `Process`:

* `<PageMeta>`
* Hero section
* Active ventures grid
* “How we choose what to build” section
* “From venture to infrastructure” tie-in → CTA to `/process`

#### A. Page meta

```tsx
<PageMeta
  title="Ventures"
  description="How Verifiable Systems Group evaluates, funds, and matures ventures from early experiments into governed, verifiable infrastructure."
  preloadImage="/hero-texture.png"
/>
```

**Overline:**

> Ventures at VSG

**Heading:**

> Infrastructure-scale ideas, tested as ventures.

**Body:**

> We don’t ship random apps. Every venture starts as a tightly scoped hypothesis, moves through a six-stage process, and either graduates into shared infrastructure or is retired with its lessons preserved. This page is the front-door into that portfolio.

#### D. Section: “How we decide what to build”

Short, “board-ready” explanation of your filter.

**Heading:**

> How ventures earn a slot in the portfolio

**Body (paragraph + bullets):**

```text
We treat portfolio slots as scarce. New ventures compete with existing work for capital, attention, and risk budget. To earn a place here, a venture has to clear three gates:

- Strategic fit — Does it reinforce our purpose of building verifiable systems and non-custodial rails?
- Risk surface — Can we bound the regulatory, operational, and capital risk in a way we’re willing to own?
- Evidence of pull — Is there real demand from the institutions or ecosystems we serve, not just internal curiosity?
```

This mirrors guidance that good B2B pages should emphasize **problem/fit** and decision criteria, not just features.

## 2. Helios detail page (`/ventures/helios`)

Helios should feel **institutional and governed**, not “degen trading engine”. We’ll echo how real companies talk about BTC treasuries: as long-term, conservative corporate assets, not speculative yield schemes.

### 2.1 Content model

* `<PageMeta>`
* Hero (what Helios is in one breath)
* 3-column “Mandate / What Helios is not / Where it lives”
* Section: “What Helios makes possible”
* Section: “Governance & risk posture”
* Section: “Who Helios matters for”
* CTA block to `/standards` or `/contact`

### 2.2 Page meta

```tsx
<PageMeta
  title="Helios"
  description="Helios is VSG’s internal capital engine — a governed system for funding research, infrastructure, and resilience from our own balance sheet, not client deposits."
  preloadImage="/hero-texture.png"
/>
```

### 2.3 Hero

**Overline:**

> Internal venture — capital systems

**Heading:**

> Helios: VSG’s internal capital engine.

**Body:**

```text
Helios is how we put our own balance sheet to work. It allocates VSG’s capital into long-term reserve assets and selective borrowing programs so we can fund research, infrastructure, and resilience without ever touching client deposits or offering yield products.
```

Tone: calm, matter-of-fact, no hype.

### 2.4 3-column “Mandate / Not / Where it lives”

Use a 3-column grid (`md:grid-cols-3`) with small cards or callouts.

**Column 1 – Mandate**

> **Mandate**
> Fund long-horizon work that would be hard to finance on a quarter-to-quarter budget: deep research, security reviews, infrastructure, and shared tooling across ventures.

**Column 2 – What Helios is not**

> **What Helios is not**
> Helios does not pool client funds, accept deposits, run a public fund, or market yield products. It exists solely to manage VSG’s own capital under governed limits.

**Column 3 – Where it lives**

> **Where it lives**
> Helios sits inside VSG’s internal stack alongside our treasury, risk, and engineering functions. It is designed to be explainable to auditors, banking partners, and regulators.

This mirrors how responsible BTC-treasury companies position their strategies—clear separation from customer funds, long-term posture, and emphasis on explainability.

### 2.5 Section: “What Helios makes possible”

**Heading:**

> What Helios makes possible for VSG

**Body + bullets:**

```text
Helios is not about chasing short-term trades. Its job is to make the rest of the portfolio more resilient:

- Smoother funding — Provide a buffer so high-value ventures are not shut down solely because external markets are noisy.
- Shared infrastructure — Pay for common security, observability, and compliance work that benefits more than one venture.
- Real-world stress testing — Let us run controlled, capital-backed experiments on new rails before they touch client-facing systems.
```

### 2.6 Section: “Governance & risk posture”

You don’t leak mechanics; you highlight *principles*.

**Heading:**

> Governance & risk posture

**Body:**

```text
Helios operates under explicit limits set by VSG’s governance framework. Capital allocation, reserve policies, and borrowing decisions are reviewed against predefined risk tolerances, with separation between those who propose positions and those who approve them.
```

Optional short bullets:

* **Conservative by design** — Long-term reserve mindset, not high-frequency speculation.
* **Non-custodial rails** — Where we use cryptographic rails, control stays with VSG; Helios never holds client keys.
* **Audit-ready** — Every move is traceable so external reviewers can understand where risk sits and how it is controlled.

This aligns with how treasury/crypto-treasury guides emphasize governance, risk limits, and auditability rather than specific instruments.

### 2.7 Section: “Who Helios matters for”

**Heading:**

> Who Helios matters for

Short paragraph:

```text
Most institutions will never interact with Helios directly. It matters because it underwrites the stability of everything else we build.
```

Then 3 short bullets:

* **Banks & partners** — Clear separation between client flows and VSG’s own capital.
* **Auditors & regulators** — A single place to understand how balance-sheet risk is managed.
* **Customers & ecosystems** — Ventures that are less likely to be yanked back or abandoned mid-stream.

### 2.8 CTA block

At the bottom, a `NextStepBlock`:

Option 1 (to Standards):

```tsx
<NextStepBlock
  nextPage="Standards"
  description="See the principles that govern how we design, test, and operate capital systems like Helios."
  href="/standards"
/>
```

Option 2 (if you want more BD-ish):

```tsx
<NextStepBlock
  nextPage="Contact"
  description="If you’re evaluating VSG as a partner, we can share additional details under NDA."
  href="/contact"
/>
```
